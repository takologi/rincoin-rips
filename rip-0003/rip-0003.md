## Abstract

This RIP specifies a **conditional fallback emission schedule** ("Scenario III" in [1, §3.5]) that may be activated by miner signaling if specific network-health metrics signal vulnerability prior to the entry of the terminal phase defined in RIP-0002. The fallback inserts an additional fixed-reward phase of 1.0 RIN/block lasting 2,100,000 blocks (~4 years) before transitioning to the 0.6 RIN/block terminal reward.

The valve is conditional: under nominal conditions, RIP-0002's baseline schedule remains in force and this RIP imposes no obligations on consensus nodes beyond carrying the activation logic. Activation requires both an objective trigger condition (defined below) and miner signaling consensus.

## Motivation

The baseline emission schedule defined in RIP-0002 transitions from 1.0 RIN/block (Phase 6) directly to 0.6 RIN/block (terminal) at block 6,300,000. This represents a 40% reduction in nominal block subsidy at a single block boundary, occurring ~12 years after CH activation.

If at the time of approach to block 6,300,000:

1. The fee market has not matured to compensate for subsidy contraction, **and/or**
2. Network hash rate exhibits high volatility, **and/or**
3. The ratio of fee revenue to total miner revenue is below a security threshold,

then the protocol risks a hash-rate collapse at the boundary, exposing the network to 51% attack windows during the transition. Rincoin's design philosophy ([1, §3.7]) anticipates this scenario and provides for an "Adaptive Stabilization Layer" that defers the final subsidy reduction by one extension phase.

This RIP formalizes that fallback as a deployable consensus change with explicit activation criteria.

## Specification

### Modified subsidy function

When activated (per the activation procedure below), the block subsidy function is modified as follows:

```
GetBlockSubsidy_CSV(nHeight):
    if nHeight <  6300000: return GetBlockSubsidy_RIP0002(nHeight)
    if nHeight <  8400000: return  1.00 * COIN          # Extension phase (CSV-only)
    return 0.60 * COIN                                   # Terminal phase
```

The extension phase spans block range `[6300000, 8400000)`, a duration of 2,100,000 blocks (≈ 4.0 years at 60 s/block).

### Phase boundaries (when CSV is active)

| Phase | Block Range | Reward (RIN) | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 – 6 | `[0, 6300000)` | per RIP-0002 | RIP-0002 |
| **Extension (CSV)** | `[6300000, 8400000)` | **1.00** | This RIP |
| Terminal | `[8400000, ∞)` | 0.60 | This RIP (boundary shifted from 6,300,000) |

When CSV is active, the cumulative supply at terminal phase entry becomes:

```
S_CSV(8400000) = S_RIP0002(6300000) + 2100000 × 1.00
               = 31,027,500 + 2,100,000
               = 33,127,500 RIN
```

This matches `S_fix = 33,127,500` reported in [1, §3.5] for Scenario III.

### Activation procedure

Activation uses **BIP9-style miner signaling with objective trigger gating**. The procedure is as follows:

#### 1. Objective trigger window

The activation window opens at block height `T_open = 5,250,000` (one full Phase 6 epoch before the original terminal boundary, ≈ 2 years lead time). It closes at block height `T_close = 6,260,000` (40,000 blocks ≈ 27.8 days before the original terminal boundary, providing minimum activation finalization time).

Within `[T_open, T_close)`, miner signaling MAY occur if and only if **at least one** of the following objective conditions is true, evaluated over a 26,208-block (~30-day) sliding window ending at the evaluation block:

- **C1 (hash rate volatility)**: The standard deviation of the 144-block-windowed hash rate, normalized by the mean over the 30-day window, exceeds 0.40 (40%).
- **C2 (fee deficit)**: The mean ratio of total transaction fees to total miner revenue (fees + subsidy) is below 0.10 (10%) over the 30-day window.
- **C3 (subsidy share threshold)**: The mean block subsidy as a fraction of total miner revenue exceeds 0.85 (85%) over the 30-day window, indicating the network is still subsidy-dependent.

These conditions are evaluated by full nodes from on-chain data; no oracle is required. The exact computation is defined in the Reference Implementation.

If none of C1, C2, C3 is satisfied at any point in `[T_open, T_close)`, this RIP NEVER activates and the network proceeds under RIP-0002's baseline schedule.

#### 2. Miner signaling

When at least one trigger condition is satisfied, miners MAY set bit 1 in the block version field (`nVersion |= 0x00000002`). Activation requires:

- A retarget period (2,016 blocks) within `[T_open, T_close)` in which **≥ 1,815 blocks (90%)** signal bit 1, **and**
- The objective trigger is satisfied for **≥ 50% of blocks** in that retarget period (verified at lock-in).

Upon meeting both conditions, the RIP enters `LOCKED_IN` state at the end of that retarget period.

#### 3. Lock-in and activation

After `LOCKED_IN`, an additional 2,016-block grace period elapses, after which the RIP becomes `ACTIVE` at the next retarget boundary. From the activation block onward, `GetBlockSubsidy_CSV` replaces `GetBlockSubsidy_RIP0002` for all subsequent block validation.

Activation MUST occur strictly before block 6,300,000.

#### 4. Failure to activate

If `T_close` is reached without `LOCKED_IN`, this RIP enters `FAILED` state and never activates. Block 6,300,000 transitions directly to the 0.60 RIN terminal reward per RIP-0002.

### Idempotence

This RIP can activate at most once. Once `ACTIVE`, the schedule shift is permanent.

## Rationale

### Why a 4-year extension rather than a smoother taper?

A continuous taper (e.g., a linear reduction from 1.00 to 0.60 RIN over 2,100,000 blocks) would introduce per-block subsidy values that are non-aligned with satoshi precision and require additional consensus state. A discrete extension preserves the discrete-halving narrative and minimizes implementation surface area.

The 4-year duration matches the dilated halving cadence introduced in RIP-0002 and gives the fee market a full four-year window to mature before the final reduction.

### Why miner signaling rather than automatic activation?

Pure objective triggering risks activation in transient anomalies (e.g., a 30-day hash rate dip due to a regional power event) that do not reflect a sustained security concern. Miner signaling adds a human-judgment layer: miners are the parties most directly exposed to a security-budget shortfall and are best positioned to evaluate whether the objective trigger reflects a genuine threat.

Combining objective gating with signaling prevents either mechanism from activating the RIP unilaterally:

- Without objective gating, miners could activate to extend their own subsidy stream regardless of network need (rent extraction).
- Without signaling, a transient market event could activate the RIP unnecessarily.

Both must concur.

### Why bit 1 (not bit 0)?

Bit 0 is reserved for backwards-compatible future use. Bit 1 is the first available signaling bit for this RIP under standard BIP9 conventions.

### Why 90% threshold rather than 95%?

The 90% threshold (matching SegWit's BIP9 threshold rather than Bitcoin's later BIP91 threshold of 80% or BIP148's 50%) reflects the higher-stakes nature of this consensus change and the relatively long activation window. With a 2-year objective trigger window and a 4-week minimum signaling period, miners have substantial time to coordinate; 90% reflects strong consensus rather than mere majority.

### Alternatives considered

1. **Always activate (i.e., bake Scenario III into RIP-0002)** — Rejected. Pre-committing to a 4-year extension forfeits scarcity properties unnecessarily if the fee market matures on schedule.
2. **Pure governance activation (no objective trigger)** — Rejected. Empirical observation of mining-related governance (e.g., Bitcoin Cash forks) suggests pure miner discretion can lead to activation against network-wide interest.
3. **Pure objective activation (no signaling)** — Rejected. Insufficient resilience to transient anomalies, as discussed above.
4. **Multiple cascading extensions** — Rejected. Compounding extensions erode the macro-cap predictability that Customized Halving is designed to preserve.

## Backwards Compatibility

This RIP defines a **conditionally activated hard fork**.

- If never activated (the expected nominal case), this RIP imposes no consensus obligations on nodes beyond carrying the activation-detection logic. A node that does not implement this RIP and a node that does will produce identical block validation results under the baseline RIP-0002 schedule.
- If activated, nodes not implementing this RIP will reject blocks in the extension phase `[6300000, 8400000)` (because they expect 0.60 RIN subsidy and observe 1.00 RIN). All consensus nodes MUST upgrade by the end of the lock-in period.

## Reference Implementation

A reference implementation is provided as a patch series against `rincoin-sim`. Pseudocode:

```cpp
// In src/validation.cpp
CAmount GetBlockSubsidy(int nHeight, const Consensus::Params& params)
{
    // CSV active check
    if (params.fCSVActivated && params.nCSVActivationHeight <= nHeight) {
        if (nHeight < 6300000) return GetBlockSubsidy_RIP0002(nHeight);
        if (nHeight < 8400000) return 1.00 * COIN;
        return 0.60 * COIN;
    }
    // Baseline (RIP-0002)
    return GetBlockSubsidy_RIP0002(nHeight);
}

// In src/versionbits.cpp
// Deployment: DEPLOYMENT_CSV_RIP0003
//   bit: 1
//   nStartTime: T_open block timestamp
//   nTimeout: T_close block timestamp
//   threshold: 1815 (out of 2016)
//   triggered: at least one of {C1, C2, C3} during window
```

The objective trigger evaluators are implemented as policy-layer helpers that read on-chain data and return booleans. They MUST be deterministic across all conforming implementations.

## Security Considerations

### Activation sniping

A miner cartel attempting to artificially trigger objective conditions (e.g., briefly throttling hash rate to inflate C1) faces the constraint that the trigger evaluates over a 30-day window. Sustaining anomalous hash-rate behavior for ~30 days is economically expensive and visible. Combined with the 90% signaling threshold, single-cartel activation is impractical under any plausible distribution of mining power.

### Failure to upgrade

Nodes that fail to upgrade between LOCKED_IN and the activation block will be partitioned from the upgraded chain at the activation block. The 2,016-block grace period (~33 hours) is shorter than is comfortable for operator notification; future iterations of this RIP MAY extend this period by amending the activation procedure (such an amendment would itself be a separate RIP).

### Macro-cap implications

When CSV is active, the long-run supply trajectory shifts: cumulative supply at terminal entry rises from 31.03M to 33.13M RIN, but the asymptotic cap remains 168M RIN. The thermodynamic equilibrium analysis in [1, §3.5] shows that the steady-state circulating supply `C^ss = r_f / γ ≈ 20.87M RIN` is identical under Scenario II and Scenario III (since `r_f` is determined by the terminal subsidy of 0.6 RIN, not by the extension phase). Therefore, CSV activation perturbs the supply *trajectory* but not the long-run *equilibrium*.

### Interaction with PoR

The extension phase delays the cessation of mining-driven issuance, which in turn delays the activation horizon for the Proof of Rinne reincarnation cycle (RIP-0005). The PoR mechanism is parameterized by `t_max` (mining cessation year); activation of CSV shifts `t_max` from ~446 years to ~450 years. RIP-0005 implementations MUST consult chainstate to determine the effective `t_max` rather than hardcoding a value.

### Replay protection

Not applicable: this RIP introduces a hard fork only on the upgraded chain. No coexisting non-upgraded chain is anticipated post-activation.
